# HALOS Concept Prompts: Tri-Layer Orbit

## Direction

The Tri-Layer Orbit direction treats HALOS as a framework coordinating distinct but related layers. The three arcs can suggest human, agent, and artifact, or principles, governance, and provenance.

## Prompt 1

```text
Minimal symbolic logo for HALOS, dark navy background, three luminous orbital arcs in cool cyan and blue moving around a stable central point, balanced and precise geometry, each orbit distinct but coordinated, subtle glow, calm institutional tone, visually representing layered collaboration within a principled framework, no text, no photorealism, no generic tech mesh.
```

## Prompt 2

```text
Framework identity mark for HALOS (Human–Agent Lineage and Origin Standard), deep midnight blue field, three elegant offset arcs orbiting a central core, refined cyan and ice-blue highlights, ordered and architectural composition, representing human, agent, and artifact in relation, modern but restrained, suitable for open governance documents and repository identity, no words or letters.
```

## Prompt 3

```text
Sophisticated icon for an open human-agent collaboration framework, square composition, a stable center surrounded by three segmented orbital bands, cool luminous palette of cyan, pale blue, and soft silver-blue, precise geometry and visible spacing, should suggest governance, provenance, and interaction among distinct layers, not flashy, not commercial, not gaming or crypto styled.
```

## Notes

- The three layers should feel coordinated, not chaotic.
- The center should remain visually important.
- Avoid excessive motion cues that make the mark feel unstable.
